Richard Ruan

Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard


Hi! I'm currently a Research Associate at the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard, and I work on developing assays to understand cardiovascular disease as part of the Ellinor Lab.

I recently graduated from the University of California, Berkeley with a B.S. in Bioengineering and a thesis on a common feature of Dengue and SARS-CoV-2 pathogeneses. I'm passionate about vascular biology, regenerative medicine, medical resource allocation, and good design.

Aside from being a workaholic in lab, I enjoy origami (modular and tesselations), writing overly passionate restaurant reviews on Yelp, practicing Studio Ghibli covers on the piano, converting people to Notion, and drinking several liters of coffee every day.
