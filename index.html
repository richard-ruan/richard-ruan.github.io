About Me

Hi! I'm Richard (he/him/his).
I'm a Research Associate at the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard currently researching cardiovascular disease as part of the Ellinor Lab. I recently graduated from UC Berkeley with a B.S. in Bioengineering, and I'm aiming to attend medical school in the future.

I'm deeply passionate about research in disease pathogenesis, regenerative medicine, novel therapeutics, and systems biology; and I've been involved in projects ranging from developing new glioblastoma treatments to understanding SARS-CoV-2 Spike pathogenesis. I've held positions at UC Berkeley (Infectious Diseases and Vaccinology), UCSF (Neurosurgery, Pulmonary Immunology), Children's Hospital Oakland Research Institute, and USC (Stem Cell).

I'm also inspired by healthcare startups, especially ones that demonstrate how good design can bring people closer to their health information. I'm currently working on a variety of projects aiming to connect members of underserved communities to new health resources.
