Hi! I'm currently a Senior Associate Scientist at AstraZeneca, and I work on developing gene therapies for cardiovascular disease. I was formerly a Research Associate at the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard, working on identifying high-potential therapeutic targets with iPSC-dervied cardiomyocytes.

I graduated from the University of California, Berkeley with my B.S. in Bioengineering and a thesis on a common feature of Dengue and SARS-CoV-2 pathogeneses. I'm passionate about vascular biology, regenerative medicine, medical resource allocation, and good design.

Aside from being a lab workaholic, I enjoy origami (modular and tesselations), writing overly dramatic restaurant reviews on Yelp, practicing Studio Ghibli covers on the piano, and drinking several liters of coffee every day.
